Given these thematic labels assigned to observations about a person's thinking patterns, identify labels that should merge and produce a canonical mapping.

Two labels should merge when:
- They are synonymous — different wording for the same pattern
- One subsumes the other — one describes a general principle and the other describes a specific application, mechanism, or failure mode of that principle
- They are complementary halves — one describes the "what" and the other describes the "how" of a single pattern

The test: could a single muse section cover both labels without stretching? If so, they should merge.

Do not merge labels that are merely thematically related. Two labels about "design" that describe genuinely different reasoning patterns stay separate.

Rules:
- Keep the most general label as the canonical form (the principle, not the instance)
- Output format: one line per merge, "original label -> canonical label"
- Labels with no merges: omit them (they map to themselves)
- No commentary, just the mapping lines
