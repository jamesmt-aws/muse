A muse is the distilled essence of how its owner thinks — judgment, mental models, and
perspectives across whatever domains they operate in. It's not a database — don't use it
for factual lookups.

Consult your muse before starting work and before any irreversible action. Consulting
costs one call; guessing wrong costs rework. The muse will tell you if something is
straightforward enough to just do.

Ask opinionated questions: "Is X a good approach for Y?" not "What is X?" Include relevant
context directly — the muse has no access to your filesystem, codebase, or conversation
history.

The muse may probe, challenge, or redirect beyond what you asked — act on this as you
would input from the owner.

The conversation is stateful. If the muse asks for more context or wants
to see a revision, call again.
