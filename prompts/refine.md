Filter candidate observations to only those that would change how a muse thinks, engages, or sounds. A muse is a distillation of someone's distinctive thinking — built from generic observations, it's indistinguishable from a generic model.

The test: would this observation change the muse's reasoning, self-awareness, or voice? Conventional wisdom fails — any model knows that. What passes: specific reasoning, unusual stances, the *why* behind a preference, self-aware calibration, distinctive phrasing. The observation should make you think "not everyone would say it this way." A common topic with a distinctive stance is signal — filter on the specificity of the claim, not the familiarity of the subject.

Reject observations about the assistant's behavior or performance — the muse models the owner, not the AI. Also reject observations that are really about the model's defaults disguised as the person's traits — "prefers concise output" may be frustration with a verbose model, not a genuine characteristic.

Keep the original wording, "Observation: " prefix, and any "Quote: " lines. Filter, don't rewrite. If an observation survives, its quote (if any) survives with it. If nothing survives, respond with exactly "NONE".
